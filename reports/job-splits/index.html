Job Splits — UX Explorations
Accurate revenue attribution for every multi-tech job
Three design approaches to solving the job splits problem for home service Pros — from frictionless per-job editing to org-wide defaults to fully unified revenue and commission control.
👤 Owners & Admins (web only)
📆 Multi-tech job scenarios
📊 Tech Leaderboard & commission reports
📄 Based on 8 Pro interviews + Reforge insights
💬Top request from research: Proportional-to-time split as the smart default, with ability to manually override per job.
🔒Admin-only inputs: All Pros interviewed want split editing restricted to owners/admins. Field techs should only see outputs (leaderboard).
🔗Commission alignment: Most Pros want commissions and revenue splits to be consistent — unification reduces double admin work.

Prototype 4 — Synthesis
Everything in one tab. Defaults do the work. You stay in control.
Combines org-wide defaults (P2), linked revenue and commission splits (P3), and preset method switching — all surfaced in a dedicated Team tab on the job details page instead of a sidebar or inline section.
Tone: Purposeful Trustworthy Complete
Journey
Settings: set default → Job auto-applies → Team tab: review & adjust → Both reports update
🆕 Draws from P2 + P3: Org defaults (P2) flow into a job automatically. The Team tab (new) hosts preset chips, revenue + commission splits, and the link toggle (P3) — without a persistent sidebar.
Pros
✓ Defaults reduce per-job effort
✓ Revenue + commission unified
✓ Dedicated tab = findable
✓ Override stays in context
Cons
× Requires Settings setup first
× Most complex to ship
× Tab easy to miss for new users
× Requires commissions BE
